Relevant projects

Elevate
This was a 8 month long research project from my Bsc. where my team and I created Elevate: an improved and cheaper alternative to the state-of-the-art cognitive assessment tool for individuals with Down syndrome. The project was developed using human-centric design and iterative design principles and tested by actual users. The primary challenge was testing the product with children with Down syndrome since they were uncomfortable in a new environment.
3D kadaster
The goal of the project was to develop a 3D model of all buildings of the Netherlands. It was a highly technical project and primary challenge coming from the scale and size of the data. It was necessary to minimize operations on pointcloud data and use efficient search algorithms to reduce the computation and time complexity.
Omniart
The goal of the project was to create an interactive system to visualize sentiments in artistic paintings. The emphasis of this project was on the design and responsiveness of the system. The main challenge was to constantly evaluate the design of the product since we were trying to express a lot of information and complex concepts on a single screen and make sure that the end users were able to understand the info being provided.
km3net
As part of my Msc. thesis, I worked with uva, escience and nikhef to develop a data processing pipeline for the km3net neutrino telescope which is being built at the bottom of the Mediterranian sea. The pipeline essentially conducts signal processing to remove the noise from the data while preserving only neutrino events. Managing the project was definitely the biggest challenge. I had to communicate with 3 different stakeholders each with varying levels of understanding. It was crutial to communicate clearly and adhere to project management best practices.

Problem statement

XR generates more data than traditional media. This makes it difficult to transmit in its entirety due to limitations posed by the network bandwidth. Thus the first challenge is to perform signal processing in order to determine important aspects of the data. The second challenge is to optimise the QoE within the constrains posed by the network leading to advancements in adaptive streaming and compression techniques.

If asked why there is a lack of trust

  1. the "black box" nature (we know how the internals work but we do not know why acertain number of neurons, number of layers, choice of activation function works for a given dataset)
  2. lack of generalizability (we have to keep training network with new data, risks of catastrophic forgetting)
  3. lack of ability to apply knowledge (NN train with bodyparts of human anatomy cannot identify body parts of other animals).

A challenge I encountered, overcame and lessons learned

One challenge I encountered while working on my Thesis was properly evaluating my models. The dataset I was working with was highly skewed (more examples of negative class compared to positive class). However the sheer size of the dataset amplified the problem to an extent that the recommended metrics for dealing with skewed data also failed to evaluate the model correctly.

The solution was to dig a bit deeper and fall back to a primitive evaluation metric which is the confusion matrix. This allowed me to identify the root cause of the problem.

It can be easy to fall into a automated workflow once we acheive a feedback loop. However, sometimes it is necessary to take a step back and evaluate the feedback loop itself since it can contain pitfalls as well.
