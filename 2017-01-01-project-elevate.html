This document summarises project Elevate which started in Fall 2017 and concluded in Summer 2018 as part of my Bsc. capstone project.

Problem Statement

The state-of-the-art cognitive assessment tools are expensive, time consuming and viewed as effortful and frustrating by users with cognitive impairement. Due to the above limitations, children and young adults with Down Syndrome only get evaluated once, or twice a year. Due to limited testing and long feedback loop, it is difficult for the parents/guardians to track their ward's progress.

The goal of Elevate was to make the cognitive tests short and extremely engaging for the primary users (children and young adults with Down syndrome). For the secondary users (parents or guardians) the goal was to provide accurate test results and reducing the cost.

Solution

A game based assessment system that cosely mimics the standardised test parameters using web technologies was created. The product was developed iteratively, and tested by real users. The objective of the project was to reduce the delta between the scores obtained in the standardised tests and our tests.

Limitations

Although our product closely mimics the test parameters of the standardised tests, we expect there to be a stigma against it since it is not a medically certified.

Only 3 of the 100 families we reached out to, accepted our invitation to test our product. Test results obtained from our primary users may not be reliable as we observed that they were often stressed or uncomfortable in the new environment (our university offices).

Future Work

Based on adaptive learning, we wish to curate the tests based on the personal preference of the primary users. Initial thoughts are to present the users with a short survey (which they are expected to complete with aid from the secondary users) which tells us more about their likes and dislikes.
